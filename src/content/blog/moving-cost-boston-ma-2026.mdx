Boston move pricing in 2026 still revolves around labor hours, access, and season—not a single flat number. Understanding how [local movers](/local-moving) build your bill helps you budget realistically and spot quotes that are too low to be safe.

## How local Boston moves are priced

Most residential moves within Greater Boston use an hourly rate for the crew and truck, plus **travel time** from the carrier’s location to your home and back after drop-off. Labor on site is also commonly billed in fifteen-minute increments. A typical team is two to four movers depending on home size and inventory.

Ask your estimator:

- How many movers are included?
- Is there a minimum hour charge?
- How is travel time measured?
- What equipment (dollies, floor protection, wardrobe boxes) is included?

Review published [pricing](/pricing) pages, then confirm with a written quote tied to your inventory.

## Sample local budgets (2026 ranges)

These ranges are illustrative for planning—not guarantees. Your home’s stairs, parking, and packing level will move the final number.

| Move type | Typical crew | Ballpark range |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Studio / 1-bed | 2 movers | $600 – $1,200 |
| 2-bed apartment | 3 movers | $900 – $1,800 |
| 3-bed house | 3–4 movers | $1,400 – $2,500+ |
| With partial packing | +1–2 hrs | Add $300 – $800+ |

Walk-ups in Brighton, Dorchester, or Somerville without elevators often run longer than suburban split-levels with driveways. Mention every flight of stairs during the survey.

## Line items that raise the total

**Access:** Long carries from a distant parking spot, narrow stairwells, or shuttle trucks when a 26-foot box truck cannot fit on your street.

**Timing:** Last-week-of-month Saturdays, September 1, and holiday weekends are peak. Mid-week dates often save an hour or more of billable time.

**Services:** [Full or partial packing](/packing-services), furniture disassembly, appliance disconnects (where offered), storage, and specialty items (pianos, safes).

**Fees:** Parking permits in Boston, tolls, fuel surcharges on long local hauls, and COI processing for condos.

## Flat rates and “not to exceed” quotes

Some Boston carriers offer flat prices after a detailed inventory. These can protect you from hourly overruns if the scope stays the same. Changes on move day—extra rooms, added heavy items, or a second stop—may trigger revisions. Read what voids a flat quote.

## Long-distance and interstate costs

Moves from Boston to another state are priced on weight (or cubic feet), distance, and access at both ends—not hourly tables. Binding estimates, shuttle trucks, and storage in transit may apply. See our [long-distance moving guide](/blog/long-distance-moving-guide-boston) and [interstate moving service](/interstate-moving) overview.

## Compare quotes fairly

When evaluating estimates:

1. Match crew size and truck type.
2. Confirm travel time policy.
3. Note included insurance (released value vs full value).
4. Verify license (Massachusetts DPU for intrastate).
5. Check reviews and complaint history.

Avoid cash-only operators with no written contract—they are a common source of hostage-load scams (see our [scam avoidance guide](/blog/moving-scams-to-avoid-boston)).

## Save without cutting corners

- Declutter 10–20% of volume before packing.
- Move Tuesday–Thursday when possible.
- Stage boxes near the entry; label rooms clearly.
- Reserve elevator and parking early.
- Use pro packing only where it matters (kitchen, TV, art).
