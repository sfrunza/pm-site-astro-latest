Eight weeks is the sweet spot for most Greater Boston moves—long enough to book quality [local movers](/local-moving), short enough to stay focused. This checklist aligns with how Massachusetts buildings, utilities, and peak-season demand actually work.

## 8 weeks out: decisions and dates

- Set your target move date; note backup dates if renting.
- Request estimates—[book](/book) after comparing licensed carriers.
- Create a folder for quotes, COI templates, and lease docs.
- Start decluttering room by room; sell or donate furniture early.
- Notify landlord or listing agent of move-out timing.

## 7 weeks out: logistics and records

- Order packing supplies or schedule [partial packing](/packing-services).
- Research school transfers if moving towns in MA.
- Photograph valuable items and note serial numbers.
- Check homeowners or renters insurance for move coverage.
- Begin a change-of-address list (USPS, bank, payroll).

## 6 weeks out: building coordination

- Ask **both** buildings for move rules, fees, elevator hours.
- Request COI template; send to your mover for processing.
- Reserve freight elevator slots at origin and destination.
- Ask about parking permits or loading dock access in Boston/Cambridge.
- Confirm whether you need a move-out/move-in inspection.

## 5 weeks out: packing wave one

- Pack off-season clothes, books, décor, and guest rooms.
- Label boxes with room and contents summary.
- Use small boxes for heavy items—critical for stairwalks.
- Back up computers; organize important documents to carry with you.

## 4 weeks out: utilities and services

- Schedule electric/gas/internet at new address; stop old service.
- Hire cleaners for old unit if required by lease.
- Transfer prescriptions to a pharmacy near your new home.
- Update pet licenses and vet records if changing towns.

## 3 weeks out: packing wave two

- Pack most of kitchen (keep daily essentials).
- Disassemble furniture you can handle safely; bag hardware.
- Confirm mover crew size, truck type, and payment methods.
- Review [moving cost factors](/blog/moving-cost-boston-ma-2026) against your quote.

## 2 weeks out: confirmations

- Reconfirm arrival window and building elevator times with mover.
- Submit COI to property managers; get written approval.
- Post parking signs or apply for City of Boston permit if needed.
- Change USPS address; forward mail start date.
- Empty storage unit items you want on the truck.

## 1 week out: essentials and disconnection

- Pack open-first boxes: toiletries, chargers, sheets, coffee.
- Defrost refrigerator; plan food use-up or donation.
- Drain garden hoses; pack toolkit for reassembly.
- Arrange childcare or pet care for move day if helpful.
- Read [prepare home for moving day](/blog/prepare-home-moving-day-boston).

## Move week: final 48 hours

- Confirm weather plan for snow or heat.
- Set aside cash or payment method per contract.
- Take meter readings and photos of empty unit.
- Keep phone charged; share building contact with crew lead.
- Walk through after load; note any damage concerns immediately.

## After move: first week in the new place

- Unpack kitchen and beds first.
- Test smoke detectors and locate water shutoff.
- Update driver’s license and registration within MA timelines.
- Leave reviews for reliable vendors; save your inventory list.
