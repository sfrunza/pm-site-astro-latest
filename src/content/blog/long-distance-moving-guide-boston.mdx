Leaving Greater Boston for another state is a different project than a Brookline-to-Quincy local job. Weight-based pricing, delivery spreads, and federal rules replace hourly tables. This guide walks you through a smooth [interstate move](/interstate-moving) from planning to delivery.

## Local vs long-distance: key differences

| Topic | Local Boston move | Interstate from Boston |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Pricing | Hourly labor + travel | Weight/cube + distance |
| Regulation | MA DPU licensing | FMCSA + USDOT numbers |
| Timing | Usually same day | Delivery window spread |
| Estimate | Often phone/video | Inventory + binding options |

Treat interstate quotes as formal procurement—not a quick text estimate.

## Step 1: Inventory everything early

Walk each room with video. Note basement, attic, and garage. List bulky items: pianos, kayaks, safes. Under-estimated weight is a top source of invoice disputes.

Declutter before survey—weight drives cost. Donate furniture you will replace in the new climate.

## Step 2: Understand estimate types

**Non-binding:** Final cost can exceed estimate if weight is higher.

**Binding:** Price fixed if inventory matches—changes if you add items.

**Not-to-exceed:** Pay the estimate or actual, whichever is lower (when offered).

Get terms in writing. Compare [Boston cost concepts](/blog/moving-cost-boston-ma-2026) but apply interstate rules, not hourly math.

## Step 3: Packing choices

- **Full pack:** Fastest, lowest damage risk for fragiles.
- **Partial:** Pros pack kitchen/TV; you handle clothes.
- **Self-pack:** Cheaper if boxes are sturdy and labeled; poor packing voids protection.

Our [room-by-room packing guide](/blog/room-by-room-packing-guide-boston) still applies—materials matter more on long hauls.

## Step 4: Dates, storage, and gaps

Closings and lease dates rarely align. Ask about **storage in transit** rates and how long goods sit before linehaul. Know fees for each day in storage.

Summer routes from Boston fill early—book spring if you can.

## Step 5: Move day at your Boston home

Same prep as local: [move-day checklist](/blog/prepare-home-moving-day-boston), parking, elevator COI. Interstate crews may use spreaders or linehaul partners—confirm who loads vs who delivers.

Keep jewelry, documents, and laptops with you. Photograph condition of high-value pieces.

## Step 6: Delivery and walk-through

At destination:

- Check inventory against load notes
- Note damage on delivery receipt before signing
- Unload essentials first; inspect boxes marked fragile early

Delivery windows are spreads—not single-hour appointments—for many interstate loads.

## Avoid scams on interstate routes

Nationwide broker fraud is common. Verify USDOT/MC, read reviews, and avoid huge upfront cash. See [10 moving scams](/blog/moving-scams-to-avoid-boston).

## Boston-specific tips before you leave

- Forward USPS mail; update RMV license within MA rules if still resident temporarily
- Cancel utilities on overlap timeline
- Return T passes or library books
- Schedule final cleaning for lease deposit
