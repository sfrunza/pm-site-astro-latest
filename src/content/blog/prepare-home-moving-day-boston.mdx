Move-day prep in Boston is about access: parking, elevators, and clear paths matter as much as packed boxes. Do this work before [local movers](/local-moving) arrive and you will shorten billable hours and reduce damage risk.

## One week before: confirmations

- Reconfirm arrival window and crew size with your carrier.
- Verify COI approval and elevator times at **both** buildings.
- Post parking signs or confirm Boston permit pickup.
- Share property manager phone numbers with the office.
- Review payment method and [pricing](/pricing) terms.

## Night before: home setup

- Finish sealing boxes; nothing half-open on load day.
- Defrost fridge/freezer; dry trays.
- Charge phones; print parking permit if required.
- Pack open-first bags for each family member.
- Plan coffee and simple breakfast—stores may be packed.

## Morning of: clear and protect

**Paths:** Remove floor clutter, hallway runners that slip, and door mats. Prop doors open where safe. Unlock interior doors crews need.

**Floors:** Many condos require protection—runners, cardboard, or adhesive mats in high-traffic paths. Protect elevator sills and lobby corners.

**Parking:** Cone off space; meet crew if street is confusing. For alleys, note low branches and tight turns—Boston’s older streets surprise out-of-town drivers.

**Kids and pets:** Off-site when possible; otherwise a closed safe room with adult supervision only.

## Staging boxes and furniture

- Stack boxes near the entry, labeled by room.
- Fragiles marked; heavy small boxes on bottom of stacks.
- Disassemble agreed items; bag hardware on furniture.
- Keep paths wide—do not block stairs with loose items.

If you used our [room-by-room packing guide](/blog/room-by-room-packing-guide-boston), staging is mostly done—just consolidate by the door.

## Items to keep with you

Documents, prescriptions, jewelry, spare keys, chargers, and day-one toiletries ride in your car. Movers commonly decline hazmat, propane, paints, and plants.

## Communicate with the crew lead

Walk through special items: antique desk, glass table, gym equipment. Point out scratches already on walls for everyone’s clarity. Note last-minute items **before** load starts—changes can affect time and price.

## Final walk-through

After the truck leaves:

- Check closets, cabinets, attic, basement, shed
- Bathroom vanities and medicine cabinets
- Remote controls and garage openers
- Take photos of empty unit for deposit return

Sign inventory if your move includes one; note concerns immediately—not weeks later.

## Weather contingencies

Spring rain: plastic wrap for upholstery; towels at doors. Winter: salt walks; clear ice on steps. Summer: start early; hydrate crew if you offer water (appreciated, not required).
