## How Interstate Moving Costs Are Calculated

The Federal Motor Carrier Safety Administration (FMCSA) regulates interstate moves. Companies use **actual weight** as the primary pricing factor:

**Weight-Based Process:**
1. Driver weighs empty truck at certified scale
2. Truck weighed again after loading
3. Loaded weight minus empty = your shipment weight
4. Rate per hundredweight (CWT) × total weight = cost

**Example:** 6,500 lb shipment at $75 per CWT = 65 CWT × $75 = $4,875 base cost.

**Estimate Types:**

- **Binding:** Price locked based on inventory. Safest if you disclosed all items accurately.
- **Non-Binding:** Final bill reflects actual weight, can exceed estimate by 10%+.
- **Binding Not-to-Exceed:** You pay estimate OR actual cost, whichever is lower (rare but best).

## Factors That Affect Your Moving Cost

Beyond base weight and distance, several variables influence your final cost:

**Time of Year:** Peak season (May-September) adds 20-35% to costs. Off-peak (October-April) saves $1,200-$2,500 on average per American Moving & Storage Association data.

**Packing Services:**
- Full packing ($1,800-$2,500 for 3BR): Fastest, lowest damage risk
- Partial packing ($800-$1,400): You pack basics, pros handle fragiles
- Self-packing ($200-$600 materials): Lowest cost, higher risk, limited coverage

**Storage:** If your Florida home isn't ready, Storage in Transit costs $150-$300/month plus $600-$1,200 loading fees. For gaps over 60 days, self-storage ($80-$250/month) may be cheaper.

**Insurance:** Basic liability ($0.60/lb) is inadequate. Full-value protection costs 1-2% of declared value—$500-$1,000 for a $50,000 shipment—but provides real protection.

**Access Challenges:** Walk-up apartments, narrow streets requiring shuttles, limited parking, and restricted-access communities increase costs. Long carry fees apply when trucks can't park within 75 feet.

## Ways to Save Money on a Boston to Florida Move

Strategic planning reduces expenses without sacrificing quality:

**1. Move Off-Peak (Save $1,200-$3,500):** October-April offers 15-30% lower rates. Target January-February for best pricing.

**2. Reduce Weight (Save $800-$2,500):** Every 1,000 lbs eliminated saves $450-$750. Sell furniture you'll replace, donate unused items.

**3. Hybrid Packing (Save $600-$1,800):** Pack basics yourself, hire pros for kitchen/electronics/fragiles. Balances cost with protection.

**4. Compare Quotes (Save $500-$2,000):** Get three binding estimates. Avoid unusually low quotes, large deposits, and companies refusing surveys.

**5. Book Early (Save $300-$800):** Reserve 6-8 weeks ahead. Last-minute moves cost 15-25% more.

**6. Flexible Dates (Save $400-$1,200):** Specific dates require dedicated trucks at 25-40% premium. Accept 5-7 day windows for standard rates.

**7. Accurate Inventory (Save $200-$1,000):** Get binding estimates with thorough surveys including attic, basement, and garage.

**Combined potential:** $3,200-$7,000 using multiple strategies.

## Why People Are Moving from Boston to Florida

According to U.S. Census Bureau data, Massachusetts to Florida migration increased **23% between 2020 and 2025**, with 18,000-22,000 people making this move annually. Key drivers include:

**Tax savings:** Florida has 0% state income tax versus Massachusetts' 5% rate. A household earning $150,000 saves $7,500 annually—$150,000 over 20 years.

**Lower housing costs:** Median Boston home ($725,000) versus Tampa ($385,000) or Orlando ($365,000). Selling Boston property and buying in Florida often leaves $200,000-$400,000 in equity.

**Weather:** Boston's 30°F winters versus Florida's 65-75°F year-round climate attracts residents seeking outdoor lifestyles without snow removal.

**Remote work:** Technology and financial services workers keep Boston salaries while enjoying Florida's lower cost of living and zero income tax.

**Considerations:** Florida has higher home insurance ($2,000-$5,000 annually), HOA fees ($200-$600+ monthly), and hot humid summers. Research healthcare and cultural amenities before moving.
