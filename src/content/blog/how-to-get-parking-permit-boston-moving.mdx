Street parking in Boston is among the most competitive in the United States. On moving day, an unmarked curb in Allston or a metered block in Back Bay can mean a blocked truck, overtime charges, and a very stressful morning. A City of Boston moving parking permit solves all of that for $69—and takes less than ten minutes to apply for online.

This guide covers every step: who needs a permit, how to apply, what it costs, when to post the signs, and which neighborhoods demand extra lead time.

## Do you need a moving parking permit in Boston?

A temporary no-parking permit for moving is **not legally mandatory** in every Boston neighborhood—but skipping it is a gamble that regularly costs people far more than the permit fee.

**You almost certainly need one if you are moving to or from:**

- Beacon Hill, South End, Back Bay, or the Waterfront
- Allston, Brighton, or Jamaica Plain on September 1 weekend
- Any street where parking fills before 8 a.m.
- A metered block or commercial zone

**You may be fine without one if:**
- You have an off-street driveway or private parking lot at both addresses
- You are moving during off-peak hours on a quiet residential street
- Your building has a dedicated loading dock

When in doubt, apply. A permit costs $69. A tow in Boston starts at $130 plus the storage fee per day.

## How to apply online for a Boston moving permit

The official application is at **[boston.gov/moving](https://www.boston.gov/moving)**. The direct permit page is **[boston.gov/departments/parking-clerk/reserve-parking-spot-your-moving-truck](https://www.boston.gov/departments/parking-clerk/reserve-parking-spot-your-moving-truck)**.

**Online application rules:**
- Apply **at least 15 days** before your move date
- Apply **no more than 8 weeks** in advance
- Allow **up to 10 days** for the signs to arrive by mail after approval

**What you will need to fill out the form:**
1. Your move address (origin or destination, depending on where the truck needs to park)
2. The exact street and nearest cross street
3. The size of your moving truck
4. Your desired permit date and time (standard window is 7 a.m.-5 p.m.)
5. A credit or debit card for payment (Visa, MasterCard, Discover, AmEx, or pinless debit)

Once approved, the City mails you official "No Parking" signs with your permit date printed on them. These are the only signs that give your reservation legal backing.

## How to apply in person

If your move is fewer than 15 days away but at least 3 business days out, apply in person:

**Office of the Parking Clerk**
Boston City Hall, Room 721
One City Hall Square, Boston, MA 02201
Phone: **617-635-2560**
Hours: Monday-Friday (call ahead to confirm current hours)

In-person applications also allow you to apply for **up to five consecutive days** of permits—useful for large or multi-day moves. Bring the address details listed above and a payment method.

## Boston moving permit fees (2026)

| Space type | Spaces covered | Permit duration | Cost |
|---|---|---|---|
| Non-metered (residential) | 2 spaces | 1 day, 7 a.m.-5 p.m. | **$69** |
| Metered (commercial/busy) | 2 spaces | 1 day, 7 a.m.-5 p.m. | **$109** |
| Additional days (in-person only) | 2 spaces per day | Up to 5 days | $69-$109 per day |

The $40 metered surcharge covers the revenue loss for those parking meters. Streets like Boylston Street, Newbury Street, or Downtown Crossing fall into the metered category.

## How and when to post your No Parking signs

This is where many people lose the benefit of the permit—and it is completely avoidable.

**The rule:** Signs must be posted **at least 48 hours before your permit start time.**

If your permit starts at 7 a.m. on Saturday, the signs must go up by 7 a.m. on Thursday at the latest.

**Best practice:**
1. Post signs as soon as they arrive in the mail—do not wait until the day before
2. Attach them to poles, not trees (city ordinance)
3. Take a photo with a timestamp as proof of posting
4. Check the signs the morning of your move; they are occasionally knocked down or stolen

Any vehicle parked **before** the signs went up is not legally required to move. Early posting is the only way to guarantee a clear curb.

## What the permit covers—and what it does not

**Covered:**
- Two adjacent parking spaces reserved exclusively for your moving truck
- Legal protection against tickets while your truck occupies those spaces during permit hours
- Official "No Parking" signage with city backing

**Not covered:**
- Parking outside the permitted hours (before 7 a.m. or after 5 p.m.)
- Spaces beyond the two included in the permit
- Other vehicles in your moving convoy
- Any sidewalk or fire hydrant violations

If your job will run past 5 p.m., coordinate with your movers early and plan for the truck to vacate or relocate at permit expiration.

## Neighborhood-specific tips

**Allston and Brighton (September 1)**
The single busiest moving day in Massachusetts. Apply eight weeks out—the absolute maximum allowed online. Expect heavy competition for permits; some blocks reach capacity. Book a crew and permit simultaneously as soon as your lease is signed.

**Beacon Hill and Back Bay**
Narrow, one-way streets with almost no buffer parking. A permit here is essential, not optional. Confirm whether your street is metered before applying so you budget the correct fee.

**Cambridge and Somerville**
These are separate municipalities—**City of Boston permits do not apply here.** For Cambridge, visit the [Cambridge parking permit office](https://www.cambridgema.gov/iwantto/applyforaparkingpermit). For Somerville, check [somervillema.gov/moving](https://www.somervillema.gov/moving).

**Downtown, Waterfront, Seaport**
Metered and commercial zones dominate. Budget $109. Check whether your building has a loading dock—if so, you may not need a street permit at all, but you will need to reserve the dock window with property management.

**Brookline**
Another separate municipality. Contact the [Brookline Parking Division](https://www.brooklinema.gov/1293/Moving-Permits-No-Parking-Signs) for their permit process.

## How your moving company can help

Licensed Boston movers handle the permit process regularly. When you [book your move](/book) with an experienced company, ask whether they:

- Apply for the permit on your behalf
- Coordinate the permit timing with their crew arrival window
- Handle multiple address permits in a single move (when origin and destination are both in Boston)

Some companies include permit coordination in their standard service; others bill it separately. Clarify this when reviewing your [move estimate](/book) so there are no surprises on move day.

## Step-by-step summary

1. **Decide if you need a permit** — if your street has any doubt, apply
2. **Apply online at [boston.gov/moving](https://www.boston.gov/moving)** at least 15 days out (or in person at City Hall, Room 721, at least 3 business days out)
3. **Pay the fee** — $69 for non-metered, $109 for metered
4. **Receive your signs** — allow up to 10 days for mail delivery
5. **Post signs 48+ hours early** — photograph the posting with a timestamp
6. **Confirm your movers know the exact location** of the reserved spaces
7. **Check signs the morning of the move** and keep the permit document on hand
