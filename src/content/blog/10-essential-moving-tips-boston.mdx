A smooth Boston move comes down to timing, building rules, and honest inventory—not luck. Whether you are leaving a walk-up in Allston or a condo on the Waterfront, these ten essentials help you avoid the parking tickets, elevator holds, and packing surprises that cost hours on move day.

## 1. Book early—especially around lease turnover

Greater Boston's rental market clusters around September 1 and many corporate leases end in spring. [Local moving crews](/local-moving) are busiest four to six weeks before those dates. Lock your date early, then confirm a week prior. If your plans shift, ask about reschedule policies when you [book your estimate](/book).

## 2. Get a real inventory, not a guess

Phone quotes based on bedroom count alone often miss narrow staircases, long carries from rear alleys, or bulky sectionals. A video walk-through or in-home survey produces a quote that matches labor hours. Share pianos, safes, or gym equipment upfront so the right equipment and crew size are scheduled.

## 3. Sort before you pack

Decluttering before boxes arrive saves money on local hourly moves and reduces landfill runs. Donate usable furniture through Boston-area charities, sell items online, or schedule bulk pickup with your municipality. Less volume also means fewer trips up triple-decker stairs.

## 4. Label by room and priority

Write the destination room on two sides of each box. Mark “OPEN FIRST” on essentials: medications, phone chargers, toilet paper, coffee, basic tools, and a change of clothes. Color dots by floor help crews in multi-level homes place items correctly without constant questions.

## 5. Protect buildings—and your security deposit

Hardwood and tile scratch easily. Use floor runners, corner guards, and padded door jamb protectors. Many property managers require them. Take dated photos of walls and floors before and after the move for your records.

## 6. Plan parking and permits

Street parking in Boston, Somerville, and Cambridge is tight. Reserve a spot with cones and “No Parking” signs where allowed, or apply for a City of Boston moving permit when moving on busy streets. Confirm loading zones with your building—some docks are shared and time-limited.

## 7. Coordinate elevators and COIs

High-rises often require freight elevator reservations and a certificate of insurance (COI). Send the building’s COI template to your mover at least five business days ahead. Align elevator windows at both origin and destination; mismatched times are a top cause of overtime charges.

## 8. Pack fragile items like a pro—or hire help

Dishes belong in small boxes with plenty of paper; glasses need dividers. TVs travel best in original boxes or specialty crates. If you are short on time, [professional packing](/packing-services) even for kitchens and fragiles only can prevent costly damage disputes later.

## 9. Prepare for New England weather

Winter moves need cleared walks, sand or salt for ice, and plastic wrap for upholstered items going through snow. Summer moves mean hydration and early starts to beat heat in unairconditioned trucks. Keep electronics climate-safe; extreme cold or heat can damage screens and drives.

## 10. Keep important documents with you

Leases, closing papers, IDs, jewelry, and laptops should ride in your car—not the truck. Photograph serial numbers on high-value electronics. Confirm [pricing](/pricing) terms, payment methods, and any storage charges in writing before sign-off.
